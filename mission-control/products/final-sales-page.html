What is inside

Curiosity Framework Guide

The foundation. Understand how curiosity-led learning actually works, why it is more effective than school-at-home, and where AI fits in — and where it does not.

Family Curiosity Prompts

Simple prompts to help you see what your child is naturally drawn to right now — not what you think they should be interested in, but what is actually alive in them.

Interest-to-Project Planning Tools

Templates that take a current fascination and turn it into a real project, experience, or meaningful next step. This is where curiosity becomes momentum.

Experience Planning Pages

Learning happens in the world, not just on paper. These sheets help you plan real-world learning through places, people, observation, and direct experience.

Role Model Study Template

One of the most underused learning tools available: real people. This template helps your child learn through the lives of others — past or present — in a way that opens up new possibilities for their own.

Weekly Rhythm Builder

Not a schedule. A rhythm. Light enough to feel free, structured enough to keep things moving forward.

Reflection + Synthesis Tools

Where the real learning gets locked in. Prompts and pages to help your family notice what stood out, what connected, and what wants to go deeper.

Family Learning Map

A living document to track interests, projects, questions, role models, and emerging directions over time. This becomes your family’s learning story in one place.

AI Prompt Pack for Parents

A curated set of prompts you can use with AI to expand interests into projects, connect ideas across disciplines, generate better questions, plan experiences, and support reflection — without letting AI become another screen-based shortcut.

FAQ

Is this a curriculum?

No. This is not a traditional curriculum or school-at-home program. It is a practical toolkit for helping parents turn real curiosity into deeper learning.

Do I need to be homeschooling to use this?

No. It is designed for homeschooling and worldschooling families, but it can also help any parent who wants to create richer learning experiences outside conventional school structures.

Do I need to be good with AI?

No. The AI prompt pack is written for parents and is meant to make things simpler, not more technical.

Will this work for different ages?

Yes. The tools are flexible enough to use with elementary through early teen learners, especially when adapted to your child’s current interests.

Is this a lot of prep work for parents?

No. One of the goals of the kit is to reduce planning friction and help you move from interest to action more simply.
