That Sedona trail sign said “Outer Limits,” and honestly it felt like a picture of the kind of life we’re trying to build.

Jonah and Noa were standing there in the red rocks, open desert all around us, full of energy and wonder. It didn’t feel like learning was something separate from life in that moment. It felt woven right into it.

That’s a big part of why we chose this path.

I don’t want my kids to grow up thinking learning only happens at a desk, inside a system, moving from one requirement to the next. I want them to know the world is full of questions, patterns, places, stories, and invitations.

Sometimes a trail, a conversation, a rock formation, or a sign in the middle of nowhere can open up more real learning than a whole checklist ever could.

That’s the kind of life I want for our family.
